Hydroponics

Plants are grown without soil, their roots suspended in a mineral-rich water solution

Organic Farming

Focuses on natural methods and avoiding synthetic fertilizers, pesticides, and genetically modified organisms (GMOs)

Greenhouse Farming

Utilizes greenhouses to control temperature, humidity, light, and other environmental factors for optimal plant growth

Terrace Farming

Creates leveled steps on sloping land to maximize usable space and prevent soil erosion

Vertical Farming

Crops are grown in vertically stacked layers, maximizing space utilization in urban areas or controlled environments

Aquaponics

Combines aquaculture (raising fish) with hydroponics, where fish waste provides nutrients for plants while the plants filter water for the fish

Precision Agriculture

Utilizes technology like GPS, sensors, and data analysis to optimize resource use and improve decision-making for each specific area of a field

Drip Irrigation

Considered the most water-efficient method, it delivers water slowly and directly to the root zone of plants through a network of pipes with emitters or drippers

Sprinkler Irrigation

Simulates rainfall by spraying water through overhead rotating sprinklers or fixed spray heads

Subsurface Irrigation

Water is delivered below the soil surface through perforated pipes, bubblers, or emitters placed near the root zone

Micro-Irrigation

A broad term encompassing drip, micro-sprinkler, and subsurface irrigation systems, all delivering water in small quantities directly to the plant root zone

Precision Irrigation

Integrates technology like sensors, weather data, and automation to schedule irrigation based on real-time needs of the crop and soil moisture levels
